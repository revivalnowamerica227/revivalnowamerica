The Six Foundational Doctrines of Christ

Essential Christian teachings for every believer from Hebrews 6:1-2

Foundational Christian Doctrines

"Therefore, leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection; not laying again the foundation of repentance from dead works, and of faith toward God, of the doctrine of baptisms, and of laying on of hands, and of resurrection of the dead, and of eternal judgment" (Hebrews 6:1-2).

These six foundational teachings—repentance, faith, baptisms, laying on of hands, resurrection, and eternal judgment—are essential for every believer to understand to achieve spiritual maturity in the Christian faith.

Biblical Foundations

What are the six foundational doctrines of the Christian faith? Many believers, even in America, struggle to answer this question because they have not been taught these essential teachings.

Our starting point is the book of Hebrews, which outlines the six foundational doctrines of Christ: repentance, faith, baptisms, laying on of hands, resurrection of the dead, and eternal judgment.

These teachings are elementary, and every believer needs to master these basics to progress to deeper biblical doctrines for spiritual growth.

1. Repentance

Repentance, in the context of salvation, means changing one's mind about sin and God, acknowledging our need for salvation (Acts 2:36-38; Mark 1:15; Matthew 3:1-2).

It is the first step to salvation. Jesus called sinners to repentance (Luke 5:32). Repentance is a gift from God, drawing us to Him for salvation.

Learn More

2. Faith

Faith is belief, trust, and confidence in God. Without faith, it is impossible to please God, and believers must trust in His existence and promises (Hebrews 11:6).

Faith is a gift from God, not a work we perform. "We are saved by grace, through faith... Not of works, lest any man should boast" (Ephesians 2:8-9).

Learn More

3. Baptisms

The Bible describes three baptisms: John's baptism, believers' baptism, and Holy Spirit baptism (Matthew 3:11; 28:19; Acts 1:8).

Water baptism is an ordinance symbolizing our faith and identification with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection.

Learn More

4. Laying on of Hands

Laying on of hands is a ceremony for imparting the Holy Spirit, spiritual gifts, blessings, or authority, often used in ordination or healing (Acts 6:6; 8:5-17; Luke 4:40).

It signifies God's power and blessing transferred through His servants.

Learn More

5. Resurrection of the Dead

Jesus' resurrection on the third day conquered death, becoming the "firstfruits" of the dead (John 20:1-31; 1 Corinthians 15:1-23).

His resurrection assures believers of their future resurrection at Christ's return (1 Thessalonians 4:16-17).

Learn More

6. Eternal Judgment

"It is appointed unto man once to die, but after this, the judgment" (Hebrews 9:27). God has appointed a final judgment for all humanity.

Jesus Christ will judge the living and the dead upon His return (Matthew 25:31-32; John 5:22; Revelation 20:12).

Understanding Biblical Doctrines

These foundational doctrines—repentance, faith, baptisms, laying on of hands, resurrection, and eternal judgment—are the cornerstone of every believer's spiritual journey.

The book of Hebrews urges us to move beyond these basics toward spiritual maturity, exploring the depths of God's Word for a deeper relationship with Christ.
